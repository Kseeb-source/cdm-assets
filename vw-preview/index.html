Common questions

Things most people ask.

How much does a will cost?

It depends on what you need, single wills, mirror wills, or wills with trusts and complex provisions all vary. Rather than quote a misleading low price upfront, we have a free 15-minute call, understand your situation, and give you a fixed quote based on the actual work involved. No hidden fees, no upselling.

I don't know what kind of will I need, is that a problem?

Not at all. Most people don't, and that's exactly what the free consultation is for. We'll ask about your situation, your family, your home, what you want to happen, and recommend exactly what you need. Often it's simpler than people fear.

How long does the whole process take?

Most wills are drafted within 3-5 working days of your consultation. You'll then have time to review, sign with two witnesses, and we securely store a copy if you want us to. LPAs take longer to register with the government (8-12 weeks) but we handle that for you.

Are you actually regulated?

Will writing isn't a reserved legal activity in the UK, so anyone can do it. We take it seriously. Our team includes a STEP-qualified Trust and Estate Practitioner (Society of Trust and Estate Practitioners, the global professional body for inheritance and trust specialists) alongside qualified legal professionals and Society of Will Writers members. Every matter is matched to the right specialist for the work. All hold professional indemnity insurance and complete ongoing training every year.

Do I have to come into the office?

No. Most of our clients prefer phone or video consultations from home. We can also visit in person across London if you'd prefer. Whatever works for you.

What if my circumstances change later?

Life moves, and so should your will. We offer ongoing update services and yearly review prompts, so when you have another child, move house, or your wishes change, your will keeps up with your life.
